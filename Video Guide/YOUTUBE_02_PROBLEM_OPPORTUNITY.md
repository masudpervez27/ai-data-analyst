# 🎬 PART 2: THE PROBLEM & OPPORTUNITY
## Duration: 3-4 minutes | ~600 words

---

## 📍 SCREEN: You back on camera, sitting comfortably

---

### [SECTION INTRO]

*Relaxed posture. Gesturing*

**YOU:**
"Before I show you how to build an AI agent, I need to paint the picture for why this matters."

*Shift in seat slightly for visual change*

**YOU:**
"See, for the last 5 years, AI has been mostly about chatbots. Chatbots that answer questions, generate text, write emails. And don't get me wrong—ChatGPT is incredible."

*Cut to: Screen recording showing ChatGPT interface for 2 seconds*

**YOU (voiceover):**
"But chatbots have a fundamental limitation..."

---

### [THE PROBLEM EXPLAINED]

*Back to you, more animated*

**YOU:**
"A chatbot is like hiring someone to answer your questions. You ask a question. They give you an answer. That's it."

*Make a gesture like a one-directional arrow with your hand*

**YOU:**
"But what if you need someone who doesn't just answer questions? What if you need someone who can actually *do things*?"

*Pause for effect*

**YOU:**
"Like, what if you want to analyze a dataset, generate an Excel report, cross-reference information from multiple sources, and summarize everything? That's not a one-off question. That's a process."

*Screen transition: Show data analysis workflow animation (5 seconds)*

---

### [REAL-WORLD EXAMPLE]

*Back to you. Make this relatable.*

**YOU:**
"Imagine you're a manager at a company. You ask ChatGPT: 'What are our sales trends this quarter?'"

*Lean back as if thinking*

**YOU:**
"ChatGPT: 'I don't know, I don't have access to your data.'"

*Gesture of frustration*

**YOU:**
"But what if you had an AI that could:"

*Count on fingers:*

**YOU:**
"One - Access your data files

Two - Write Python code to analyze it

Three - Run that code and get results

Four - Remember what it found

Five - Use those results when you ask follow-up questions"

*Lean forward.*

**YOU:**
"That's not a chatbot anymore. That's an agent. And this is where AI gets powerful."

---

### [WHAT COMPANIES ARE DOING NOW]

*Screen shows: Screenshots of companies using AI agents (news articles, blog posts)*

**VOICEOVER (you):**
"Look at what's happening right now in 2025..."

*Show: Microsoft deploying agents, OpenAI agents framework, etc.*

**VOICEOVER (you):**
"JPMorgan is building AI agents for legal document review. McKinsey is automating consulting analysis with agents. Every major tech company has an agents team."

*Back to you on camera*

**YOU:**
"And what's wild is... most of these companies aren't using super-secret proprietary tech. They're using the same LLMs and the same concepts that are publicly available."

*Point to camera*

**YOU:**
"So the question is: If billion-dollar companies are building agents, why are you not?"

---

### [THE OPPORTUNITY GAP]

*Shift tone to inspiring*

**YOU:**
"Here's what I'm seeing: There's a massive gap between people who can *use* AI and people who can *build* AI systems."

*Screen shows: Job market graph or statistic (animated)*

**VOICEOVER:**
"The demand for AI engineers and AI automation specialists is skyrocketing."

*Back to you*

**YOU:**
"And most people don't realize—you don't need to be a PhD to build an AI agent. You just need to understand three things:

One - How to talk to an LLM (prompt engineering)

Two - How to get the LLM to do what you want (structured responses)

Three - How to execute on what the LLM suggests (code execution)"

*Tap desk for emphasis*

**YOU:**
"That's it. Those three things. And I'm going to show you all three."

---

### [THE MARKET CONTEXT]

*More data/statistic on screen*

**YOU:**
"The AI market is projected to be worth over $2 trillion in the next few years."

*Back to you*

**YOU:**
"But you don't need to see that market to win. You just need to solve one specific problem for one specific person or company."

*Gesture broadly*

**YOU:**
"Want to automate data analysis for marketing agencies? Build an agent.

Want to create an AI research assistant for academics? Build an agent.

Want to automate customer support conversations? Build an agent.

The applications are endless."

---

### [TRANSITION TO MAIN CONTENT]

*Energy builds*

**YOU:**
"And the crazy part? The foundation for all of this—the core architecture, the core concepts—they're all the same."

*Lean in*

**YOU:**
"So if you learn how to build *one* agent, you can build *any* agent. And that's what I'm teaching you today."

*Slight smile*

**YOU:**
"But before we build anything, let me make sure you understand what an agent actually is. Because if you get this concept right, everything else clicks into place."
